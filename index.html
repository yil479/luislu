MUSAIC

  • Class: COGS121, UC San Diego
  • Date: 2018/04-2018/06
  • Role: Product Design/Backend developer


Summary

Musaic is a conceptual music app that expands upon the friend's sidebar on Spotify. It runs with the idea that people want to know what their friends are listening to. People would be able to form communities and see statistics on their friends like "Most Played Song", "Most Played Artist", and etc. This helps people get a more diverse range of music into their libraries.


Solution

My team and I used to Spotify API to make the app come to life. We pulled all the user data based on what information was available, including top artists and top songs. From here, we pulled the thirty second previews as well so that users can click on each song and got their respective album covers as well. We also integrated the ability to add your friends' songs to your own library on Spotify.




Our prototype


Our app aims to build a music sharing community in which users share their favorite songs to their friends by limiting users to showcasing one song at a time in order to emphasize that this song is supposed to be meaningful to them, and something that they want to share with others.

Desktop view with Spotify login iteration


Final iteration of login screen: A logo has been added and the colors are more muted (because it clashes with the choices on the next screen). Information about the app is also included at the bottom.


Final interation of home page: Color scheme and styling changed to be more muted so eyes will not strain, pulling data from Spotify, added previews for songs by clicking on them, direct to Spotify playlist feature, categories on top, friend searching moved to community page.


Database (Google firebase)


Every time the user logs in, they should see their friends. To accomplish this, we used Firebase to host all the variables we pulled from Spotify. We would then pull from the database whenever the user logs back in and update what the user sees using Javascript.

San Diego air pollution data analysis

The air quality is one of the major concerns of many industrialized countries. Harmful or excessive amount of gasses, particulates or biological molecules will cause air pollution, which will then lead to diseases, allergies or even death to humans and living organisms. The air quality is going to be measured and communicated through the AQI(Air Quality Index). Different number and different color is corresponding to certain level of Air Pollution. Data Science Project: San Diego Traffic collision

Data science in practice

From the poor amount of lighting in the neighborhood of La Jolla, to the narrow one way streets of Downtown SD, there are numerous concerns for drivers in San Diego to make it a safer place for drivers and pedestrians alike. Our final project revolves on the following question, "Are streets in San Diego with higher traffic volumes more dangerous?". We defined dangerous in two ways: number of accidents and number of injuries.

To approach this issue, we examine a dataset that contains police reports of collisions that have happened in San Diego County over the past few years, and perform various analyses on these reported collisions. These police reports give details such as time and day of the incident, what street(s) the incident took place at, and a standardized description of the incident. We also wanted to see how related car accidents and injuries were to traffic volume, so we looked at the traffic counts dataset, which is a data set that contains the number of cars that drove through any particular street section on a given date.
